# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This documentation set describes the end-to-end OctoAcme project management workflow, from initiation and planning through execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Teams can use it as a shared operating model so project goals, responsibilities, and delivery expectations stay aligned across functions.

The process follows a practical lifecycle: define the problem and goals, plan scope and milestones, execute in iterative cycles, and release with clear verification steps. Risk management and communication are integrated throughout the lifecycle so blockers, dependencies, and stakeholder expectations are handled early instead of late.

Roles and personas are intentionally cross-functional. Product strategy and prioritization are led by the Product Manager, delivery and cadence are coordinated by the Project Manager, and Developers plus QA/Testing collaborate to meet quality and acceptance criteria. Stakeholders provide business context, decisions, and approvals at key checkpoints.

Quality assurance and release readiness are built into daily execution, not treated as a final-step handoff. Testing practices, progress tracking, risk monitoring, release checklists, and retrospectives together support reliable delivery and continuous improvement from one project cycle to the next.

## Roles

- **Project Manager (PM):** Owns delivery planning, coordination, timeline tracking, and escalation management.
- **Product Manager (PdM):** Defines outcomes, manages priorities, and aligns delivered work to customer and business value.
- **Developers:** Implement solutions, participate in estimation and design decisions, and maintain engineering quality.
- **QA/Testing:** Validates acceptance criteria, testing coverage, and release readiness across environments.
- **Stakeholders:** Provide strategic input, approve key decisions, and review progress against business objectives.

## How Team Members Should Use These Docs

1. Start with the overview to understand the lifecycle, artifacts, and shared principles.
2. Use the initiation and planning guides before kickoff to align scope, milestones, and ownership.
3. During delivery, follow execution, risk/communication, and release guides as the operational playbook.
4. After each release, use retrospective guidance to capture lessons learned and improve the next cycle.
